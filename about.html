Background & History

Bloemfontein Community Support (BCS) was founded by a group of dedicated local volunteers who saw the urgent need to uplift underprivileged communities in Bloemfontein, South Africa. Many families in the area faced challenges such as poverty, limited access to education, and food insecurity, which negatively affected children’s learning outcomes and overall well-being.

Over the years, BCS has grown into a trusted non-profit organisation working hand in hand with schools, clinics, and community leaders to provide practical solutions. Our work includes food distribution programmes, community gardens, tutoring, and skills development initiatives. By promoting sustainability and empowerment, we aim to break the cycle of poverty and give families the tools they need to thrive.

Today, BCS continues to focus on three main areas: Education Support, Food Security, and Community Development. With a strong team of volunteers and local partnerships, we are making a tangible impact in the lives of children and families across townships and rural communities in and around Bloemfontein.

Mission

To empower underprivileged communities in Bloemfontein through education initiatives, food security programmes, and community development — enhancing quality of life and promoting sustainable change.

Vision

Thriving, resilient communities where children succeed in education, families are food secure, and residents have the skills and opportunities to build a brighter future.
